## What everyone needs to know (3 sentences)

The game is a 2D maze where the player explores under a fog of war; the more they "succeed" (open chests, expand vision), the higher the Instability stat climbs and the maze itself starts to mutate. The whole game runs on one tonal rule: **calm surface, quiet wrongness inside** — the visual analog is "color thickens as instability rises," the audio analog is "dissonance, not loudness," the textual analog is "no exclamation marks." If you find yourself reaching for something punchy, loud, or cheerful — wrong direction.

## If you're the artist

### Universal art specs

- **Format:** PNG, transparent background where appropriate (all sprites; tiles can be opaque)
- **Source resolution:** 24×24 pixels per tile / sprite, no in-tile padding
- **Color mode:** 8-bit indexed or 24-bit RGB — your pick, but colors must match the palette below **exactly** (hex code, not "close enough")
- **Style:** pixel art, soft pastel shading, OMORI / Yume Nikki register. **No** outlines that are pure black (`#000000`)
- **References to study before drawing:**
  1. OMORI — WHITE SPACE / Headspace room tiles (search `omori whitespace tile`)
  2. Yume Nikki — Number Worlds / The Mall tiles (search `yume nikki number world tiles`)
  3. OneShot — Pepper's House interior (search `oneshot pepper's house pixel`)

### Palette (paste these hex codes into your editor)

```
Floor:           #F0E8DC   warm cream
Wall:            #A98DAE   muted mauve
Fog:             #2A1F2E   deep plum (NEVER #000000)
Player body:     #E89BA0   anxious coral
Reward (chest):  #F4C8A8   tempting peach
Real exit:       #B8D4C2   faded mint (low presence)
Fake exit:       #D8A8C8   pink-lavender (slightly louder)
```

### T0.7a — Tile specs

| File | Style notes |
|------|-------------|
| `assets/tiles/floor.png` | Cream `#F0E8DC` base. Subtle internal texture optional — 1–2 px tonal variation in pastel mauve, max. Must tile seamlessly with itself in a 4-direction grid. |
| `assets/tiles/wall.png` | Mauve `#A98DAE` base. Slightly darker top-edge shading (~`#8A7393`) to give vertical read. Must tile seamlessly in horizontal runs. **No** anti-aliased edges against the floor — keep hard pixel boundary. |

### T0.7b — Sprite specs

| File | Style notes |
|------|-------------|
| `assets/sprites/player.png` | Coral `#E89BA0` body, single idle frame (animation = R1). The silhouette must read against `#F0E8DC` floor by **saturation difference**, not outline. |
| `assets/sprites/chest.png` | Peach `#F4C8A8` closed chest + open variant in the same PNG (side-by-side or as a 2-frame strip). Open variant exposes a slightly desaturated peach interior — the chest gives you a thing but it does not look proud of it. |
| `assets/sprites/exit_real.png` | Mint `#B8D4C2`. Deliberately low-presence: keep contrast against floor tight (within ~30 luminance steps). The real exit **must be easy to walk past.** |
| `assets/sprites/exit_fake.png` | Pink-lavender `#D8A8C8`. Slightly more saturation than the real exit, but still pastel. The fake exit must say "look at me" without being obviously a trick. |

### Don't do

- ❌ Pure black outlines or shadows — use `#2A1F2E` plum if you need dark
- ❌ Saturation higher than ~50% on any tile or sprite (rewards/exits may push slightly higher, base palette stays muted)
- ❌ Effects that read "energetic" — no sparkles, sunbursts, motion lines
- ❌ Anti-aliasing that softens pixel edges — keep them hard

## If you're the composer

### Universal audio specs

- **Format:** OGG Vorbis, stereo preferred (mono acceptable)
- **Sample rate:** 44.1 kHz, ≥ 128 kbps
- **No** percussion in the ambient loop. Stings may use single sustained hits (no drum kits).
- **No** crescendos in any cue. The world is collapsing calmly.

### Direction (one sentence)

> *The music feels like a soft drone that thickens as the maze remembers more of you — calm at start, dissonant by the time you stop being able to leave.*

### Reference track

**OMORI — "WHITE SPACE"** (Pedro Silva). Search: `omori white space ost`.

### T0.7c — Ambient loop spec

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| File | `assets/audio/ambient_loop.ogg` |
| Length | **~30s** seamless loop (the original §6 spec said 60s; 30s is the MVP — extend in R2 if time) |
| Texture | Single sustained pastel drone (low pad) + minimal piano motif on top |
| Tempo | Free / unmetered preferred |
| Loop point | Must be **sample-perfect seamless** — no audible click at the seam |

**Stings (R1 follow-up, NOT T0.7):** the 3 instability stings and 3 ending cues from §6 are not in this first batch. Ship the ambient loop only.

### Don't do

- ❌ Any percussion — no drum kit, snare, kick
- ❌ Build-up / drop song structure
- ❌ Anything that "warns" — the music never raises its voice
- ❌ Sounds outside the pastel-drone tonal world — no synth saws, no brass
